# Annexure C — Emotional Journey Details
## Ramesh - First Personal Loan – Daily Wage Worker
Ramesh arrived at Bajaj Finserv's homepage in a state of quiet anxiety — a 29-year-old daily wage worker from Jodhpur staring at a blank light-blue screen that told him nothing, not even that the page was loading. For a man who rarely uses financial websites and was already nervous about whether someone like him could even get a loan, those first silent seconds were unsettling. When the homepage finally appeared and he spotted the 'Personal Loan' tile, his mood lifted instantly — one tap and he was on the product page, where 'No collateral and no hidden charges' hit him like a direct answer to his deepest fear. The EMI calculator became his anchor: he typed in ₹50,000, watched it update to show ₹625 per month, and felt something close to relief — that was manageable. But the calculator summary also showed a total of ₹54,043, and no matter how many times Ramesh read it, he couldn't reconcile why borrowing ₹50,000 meant owing ₹4,043 more, especially when the page had just promised no hidden charges. He kept scrolling — past a 'CHECK LOAN OFFER' button he wasn't sure about, past tables of numbers he half-understood — until he finally found the large orange APPLY button and tapped it with cautious hope. What came next undid everything: a 'Get Started' popup talking about investing and insurance, asking for his mobile number, treating him like someone who had arrived to open a financial portfolio rather than fix his leaking roof. When he dismissed it and it came back a second time, Ramesh felt the website wasn't listening to him at all — and a man who had spent the last few minutes fighting through jargon and unexplained fees had nothing left to give.
