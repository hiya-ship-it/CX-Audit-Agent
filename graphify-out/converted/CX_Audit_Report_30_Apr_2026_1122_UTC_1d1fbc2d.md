# Annexure C — Emotional Journey Details
## Ramesh - First Personal Loan – Daily Wage Worker
Ramesh arrived at Bajaj Finserv's website with the quiet determination of someone who has been worrying about a problem for weeks — his home needed repair, he needed ₹50,000, and this was his best shot. The blank screen that greeted him at Step 0 immediately tested that resolve, but he waited it out, and when 'Personal Loan' appeared in the icon strip, he felt something close to hope. Typing ₹50,000 into the calculator and seeing 'Amount to be credited in your bank: ₹50,000' reflected back was the high point of his entire journey — a brief, shining moment where the site seemed to understand exactly what he needed. But when he clicked the orange APPLY button with all that hope behind it and landed on a page full of fee tables, loan variant names he had never heard, and 'Penal charge: 36% per annum', his confidence shattered — not because the numbers were wrong, but because he had no idea why he was here when he'd clicked APPLY. The next ten steps were a grinding, jargon-filled scroll through content written for someone with an MBA, not a daily wage worker from Jodhpur; the eligibility table's 'Employed with: Public, private, or MNC' sat in his chest like a stone, making him quietly wonder whether the whole system was built for someone else. When he finally summoned the courage to click APPLY a second time and landed on the exact same wrong page again, Ramesh didn't rage — he just went quiet, the way someone does when they've realised the door was never going to open, and thought about which friend he could ask for help instead.

The eligibility criteria listed employment as 'Employed with: Public, private, or MNC' — three categories that explicitly describe formal sector employment. User Impact: Ramesh, a daily wage worker, read this and felt his stomach drop — 'Main eligible hoon ya nahi?' (Am I eligible or not?).
