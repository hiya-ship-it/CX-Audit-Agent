# Annexure C — Emotional Journey Details
## Ramesh - First Personal Loan – Daily Wage Worker
Ramesh arrived at Bajaj Finserv's homepage with cautious hope — a daily wage worker in Jodhpur who had scraped together the courage to apply for his first-ever loan to repair his home. He spotted 'Loans' in the top navigation and felt it was exactly the right door. It wasn't. The link routed him — not once, not twice, but five times across the journey — to a page about foreclosures, Flexi Loan accounts, and loan account numbers, a page written entirely for people who already had a Bajaj loan. Each time he landed there, Ramesh felt more convinced the website wasn't built for someone like him. By Step 5 he was at his wit's end, typing 'personal loan' into the search bar as a last resort. When the autocomplete surfaced 'Personal Loan' immediately, his eyes lit up — finally, a door that opened. He arrived on the right page, dismissed a confusing shopping dropdown that had appeared from nowhere, carefully typed ₹50,000 into the calculator, and watched the Loan Summary confirm his amount and show an EMI of ₹625/month — a rare moment of genuine alignment between what he needed and what the site showed him. But when he clicked the orange APPLY button with a racing heart, the calculator silently reset to ₹1,00,000 and the processing fee jumped from ₹505 to ₹4,009 without explanation, leaving him staring at the screen asking 'why does it keep changing back?' — the journey ended not with a rejection but with a loop, the system trapping a determined man in circles until he was simply stuck, his application never started, his home still unrepaired.
